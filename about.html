All About Michou Cafe

The name Michou stems from the owner's, Marie Noel, nickname Michou (Me-shoo). It was given to her by her family when she was young. Marie has always dreamed of creating a Haitian buisness and when she traveled to the United States, she often felt homesick and yearning for Haitian food. As she settled in the STL area with her children, she sought out the Haitian community and found out that there aren't many Haitian resturants in the area. So, she began to learn about the food industry and brainstorm ideas for her buisness with her only daughter, Imani. As Marie was retelling the story of she got the nickname Michou, Imani came up with the name Michou Cafe. Now Marie's dreams are slowly becoming a reality.

Marie used to walk home from school and stop by the market place. She would smell the pate in the stands and would rush home to beg her mom for money. When her mom did giv her money, she would walk to the pate stands and buy one. Walking back home, she would find herself wanting more pate. And each time she went back, they would all be sold out. As she moved to America, she found herself wanting to taste another pate, but many places did not make her want to go back for seconds. So with spices and Haitian flavor she recreated the Haitian pate that she's grew to love. Maybe you will find yourself coming back for more, but this time there will be enough for anyone.
