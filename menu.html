Appetizers

Pâté $20

The Haitian pâté is a spicy, savory meat patty seasoned to perfection. It is one of the most popular Haitian dishes and is most commonly eaten as a snack. Be cautious when only one ordering pâté because you'll soon want another!

Fried Plantain $70

Fried plantain is a sweet banana fried. This is an extremely popular Carribean dish. Many countries around the globe make fried plantain. Hatian fried plantain can be cooked two ways: sweetly or salty.

Pikliz $15

Pikliz is a spicy cabbage side dish. It uses the most common spice in Haitian dishes: the haberno pepper. This dish is best paired with heavier foods such as Red Beans and Rice or Oxtail stew.

Plates

Red Beans & Rice $30

Red beans & rice: a classic. A meal is not a meal unless there is a side of rice and beans. Haitian rice and beans can be made with a variety of beans, but the most pouplar are Pinto, red kidney beans, and black beans.

Haitian Chicken Soup $100

Haitian Chicken Soup is the perfect fufilling dish. This soup is commonly eaten during the winter season as it very hearty and warms one up during the colder days and nights of the year. This soup contains lots of veggies and healthy minerals. People who are feeling sick or ill have been known to feel better after eating Haitian Chicken Soup. It's the Haitian equivalent of chicken soup.

Haitian Mac n Cheese $50

A classic comfort food added with a cultural twist. Haitian Mac and Cheese is the tropical version of classic mac and cheese! It’s made with sauteed bell peppers and onions and it’s just as cheesy and comforting! This delicious Haitian dish is perfect as a comforting dinner or as a fun Holiday side dish.

Seasonal Dishes

Soup Joumou $500

For Haitians, soup joumou is synonymous with freedom. The story goes that during French colonial rule of Haiti, enslaved Africans were forced by their oppressors to cultivate squash for this dish but were not allowed to eat it. When Haitians won their independence on Jan. 1, 1804, they ate soup joumou to celebrate. The formula varies slightly from family to family, but usually includes calabaza squash, beef marinated in a bright epis seasoning of onions, peppers and herbs, an assortment of other vegetables and pasta.

Drinks

Haitian Cremas $100

Cremas (Kremas or Cremasse) is a popular drink in Haiti and is served regularly at social events and during the holidays. It is usually consumed along with a sweet pastry of some sort. The beverage possesses a creamy consistency similar to a thick milkshake and varies from off-white to beige in color.
